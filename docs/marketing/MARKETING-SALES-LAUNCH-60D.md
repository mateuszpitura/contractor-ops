# Marketing & sales plan — first 8 weeks (launch)

**Audience for this plan:** **Solo developer / product owner** doing GTM yourself. The doc covers **bootstraps** and a **sustained test budget (~€1–2k/mo)**—still not a full marketing team, but enough to run **serious** paid learning if you keep focus.

Companion to **`LANDING-AB-STRATEGIES.md`**.

---

## How this plan differs from a “team GTM” doc

| Team playbook | **Your** realistic default |
|---------------|----------------------------|
| Several channels in parallel | **One** core paid thesis + organic/network; at **~€1–2k/mo** add **retargeting**—not four cold channels at once |
| 20–50 qualified conversations in 8 weeks | **5–20** *meaningful* conversations can be a **win** if each teaches you something; quality and learning > volume |
| Retargeting, webhooks, G2, PR | **Retargeting** is realistic with **~€1k+/mo** and site traffic; **G2/PR** *paid* bundles stay backlog until justified |
| “Marketing FTE + sales” | **You**—so protect calendar with qualification, Loom, and **office hours** |
| Big paid to learn | **Spend to validate**, not to “feel like marketing”—at **higher** monthly spend, the same rule: **one thesis per month**, review weekly, kill losers fast |

**Your edge:** you can make the **landing, analytics, and product** credible without a dev agency; use that before you buy reach.

## Time budget (honest)

Rough **weekly** split for a solo owner (tune to your life):

- **Product & support** — majority of hours (unchanged; this plan doesn’t assume you go half-time on code).
- **GTM (total ~4–10 h/week in launch weeks):** e.g. **2 h** content/outreach, **1–2 h** calls/demos, **1 h** metrics + inbox, **1–2 h** light content or community.

If a week is **0** GTM because you’re fixing production—**that’s normal**. The plan is **directional weeks**, not a daily grind.

## Money budget (rough tiers — you pick one lane)

**Tier 0 — Bootstrap test (~€0–100/mo in tools + tiny experiments)**  
- Free tier analytics (or one cheap product), domain, email. **No** paid ads required to start. **Warm intros + organic + one great LP** = enough to get first signal.

**Tier 1 — Modest “learn” budget (~€100–500/mo)**  
- **One** small paid test: e.g. LinkedIn *or* Google Search in **one** geo, **tight** keywords/audience, **daily cap**, 2–3 creatives max. Goal = **is there any CPL you’d pay again?** not volume.

**Tier 2 — Stretched test (~€500–1k/mo, or a one-off €1k over 2 months)**  
- **Double down** on the single channel that moved **qualified** interest; or pay **one** freelancer for: short edit of your Loom, or a Polish+English pass on key LP copy—not a full agency retainer.

### Tier 3 — ~€1,000–2,000 / month

This is a **strong solo-level** budget: not enterprise spray, but enough for **statistically useful** paid learning **if** you keep moving parts few and **follow up** on every lead (otherwise you’re buying expensive silence).

**Principles**

- **One primary thesis at a time** (e.g. “PL finance leaders care about KSeF + process” *or* “Agency COOs feel pain A”)—not five campaigns with different stories.  
- **At most 2–3** active paid surfaces in a month (e.g. one core campaign + retarget, or one platform until it works, then a second). **Check weekly**; default is **one** main channel in weeks 1–2, then add retargeting or a challenger.  
- **Optimize for qualified conversations**, not CPM. A €40 CPL on a true ICP beats €2 lookers who never book.

**Example allocation (illustrative — adjust monthly)**

| Bucket | ~Share | Examples |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Paid media (test + scale)** | **~75–85%** (€750–1.7k) | LinkedIn (company size + job title + geo), and/or **Google Search** on **tight** intent in PL; avoid broad “invoice” until negatives work. |
| **Retargeting** | **~10–20%** (when pixel + enough traffic) | Meta or LinkedIn: 30s+ on site or pricing view; cap frequency; exclude customers/trials. |
| **Tools** | **~5–10%** | Plausible/PostHog, email for broadcasts, ad accounts. |
| **Creative / one-off help** (optional) | **Outside** monthly or rare | 3 static ads from a designer (€100–300), or EN/PL copy review—**not** a monthly retainer. |

**Concrete splits (hold each 2–3 weeks min before you judge failure)**

- **~€1k/mo:** e.g. **€850** to **one** channel (LinkedIn *or* Google), **€100** retarget if volume exists, **€50** tools—or **all €1k** to **one** channel until something converts.  
- **~€2k/mo:** e.g. **€1.2–1.4k** primary (2–3 ad sets testing **A vs B** or **A vs C** [from LPs](./LANDING-AB-STRATEGIES.md) + matching landers), **€300–500** retarget, **€100** tools, small buffer for creative or extra testing.

**What you can do at Tier 3 that you couldn’t on Tier 0–1**

- **Ad-level A/B** of angles with **separate** LP paths (`?variant=`) for clean measurement.  
- **PL Search** (KSeF / B2B faktury / kontraktor–style) with a **C-flavored LP** and strict **negative** keywords.  
- **Retargeting** to recover “almost” leads.  
- **Segment** test for **D** in its **own** ad set + lander, not mixed with generic.

**Still don’t throw money at:** press releases, G2/Clutch **paid** bundles, DFI outbound lists you won’t call, many countries in parallel, or **four** unoptimized platforms.

**Time:** budget only works with **~2–3 h/week** on: numbers, pausing bad ad sets, **Within one business day** lead response. If you can’t, lower spend to Tier 1.

**Pipeline (very rough):** with spend + good LP + follow-up, **~10–35 qualified touches in 8 weeks** is a plausible **stretch**—not a promise.

**What *not* to buy early (any tier):** see above; also **“LinkedIn lead gen as a service”** you can’t follow up, or multiple ad platforms you won’t optimize.

## Early revenue: reinvest in product and traction (default)

**Assumption for this plan:** in the **early stage**, **customer revenue is mostly reinvested**—not pulled out to maximise short-term personal income. The loop you want is: **revenue → better product + support + *measured* GTM** (the Tier 0–3 tests above, creative that converts, a contractor who helps you ship—not unfocused “growth hacks”).

- **Product:** close gaps that **paying** ICPs surface (onboarding, reliability, a missing integration); that **compounds** into references and lower churn.  
- **GTM / track:** use reinvestment to **sustain or scale** what already showed signal (same channel, same message, more budget or one extra surface like retargeting)—**after** you can tell which leads were real.  
- **Runway link:** early MRR can **subsidise** ad spend and tools so external runway strains less—but only when unit economics and **cash after tax/VAT/obligations** (per **your** accountant) allow. This doc is **not** tax or legal advice.  
- **Sustainable founder pay:** paying yourself **enough to stay** (health, time, not heroics) is still an investment in the business; the distinction is **reinvest in compounding** vs **optimise for extraction**—name your line.

That mindset fits a solo builder who is **gaining track** (proof, reputation, repeatability) before optimising for dividends.

## Intent of the first 60 days (solo-realistic)

- **Prove** that **one** message + one wedge resonates with a **specific** ICP (see [LANDING-AB-STRATEGIES](./LANDING-AB-STRATEGIES.md): usually **A**, **B**, or **C** in PL).
- Get **enough** conversations to learn: objections, who actually buys, what breaks in onboarding.
- **Optional:** 1–2 design partners or paying pilots—not a made-up “500 SQLs” target.

**Good outcome for many solos:** 1–2 **serious** customers or pilots, **10+** good discovery touches (mix of email, call, DMs, trials with real follow-up), and a **sheet** that shows which message/source worked.

---

## Goals (8 weeks) — OKR-style (scaled for solo)

**Objective A — Learning**  
- **2** landing experiments (even simple: A/B headline or `?v=a` / `?v=b` with manual comparison in a sheet) tied to the doc.  
- One line **“qualified lead”** definition you reuse everywhere.

**Objective B — Pipeline (adjust to your tolerance)**  
- **Target range:** **5–20** qualified conversations over 8 weeks (demo, or trial with work email you actually spoke to, or clear pilot intent). *Fewer* is fine if you’re going **high-touch**; don’t measure yourself against a 10-person sales team’s metrics.  
- If you run **~€1–2k/mo (Tier 3)** with good follow-up, treat **~10–35** touches over 8 weeks as a **stretch** band—not a floor; **quality and learning** still beat volume.

**Objective C — Assets (minimum viable)**  
- **1** product explainer: **5–7 min Loom** or 90s cut for the site (ship rough; you can re-record later).  
- **1** written piece you own (blog, LinkedIn article, or dev.to)—aligned to one variant.  
- **0 or 1** one-pager PDF (export from Notion/MD to PDF is fine). Skip if it blocks shipping.

## What you are **not** doing in the first 60 days (permission to skip)

- Running **four** ad platforms “for reach.”  
- **Webinar + podcast + blog daily** on top of building.  
- **G2/Clutch** *unless* a customer offers or it’s zero marginal effort.  
- **Retargeting** *unless* you have traffic, a clear creative, and time to run it (typical with **€1k+/mo**; optional below that).  
- **“Always on”** outbound to hundreds of cold leads (unless you *like* that and can sustain it).  
- **Hiring** marketing before you have **repeated** pain language from 10+ ICP calls.

You can add these **after** one channel and one message show life.

## Positioning → channel → LP (unchanged logic, tighter execution)

Use UTMs; send **one** angle per link. (Full table: [LANDING-AB-STRATEGIES](./LANDING-AB-STRATEGIES.md#positioning--channel--lp-variant-quick-map).)

**Solo default priority of channels (usually):**

1. **Warm network + direct outreach** (ex-colleagues, customers from past life, one accountant you know)—**highest ROI per hour**.  
2. **LinkedIn organic** (yours, low frequency, high substance).  
3. **One** community (Slack/Discord) where ICP actually is—**help first**, one soft mention.  
4. **Search or LinkedIn paid** at **Tier 1+**; at **~€1–2k/mo (Tier 3)** you can add **retargeting** and a **second** angle test once the first channel is instrumented. Still prefer **one** core geo to start.

## Eight-week view (solo cadence)

| Week | Marketing theme | Sales / founder theme |
|------|------------------|------------------------|
| 1 | Analytics + UTM, one default LP, tell **friends + network**; fix signup/calendar | Qualification: 3 questions before a call; 15–20 min call **max**; Loom for repeat questions |
| 2 | **2** LinkedIn posts; **paid** on if Tier 1+ (e.g. **€200–500/wk** at Tier 3); **1** short post or email | Personal follow-up to any signup; start objection note |
| 3 | A/B: two heroes **or** two sources (e.g. PL vs network)—don’t test 5 things | Batch demos **one day** if possible; protect build days |
| 4 | **Double down** on what got replies (even if that’s DMs, not “scalable”); **trim** any spend with zero qualified | Review: 30 min, sheet only |
| 5 | **Second** content piece *or* refresh Loom with real UI; optional **async** “office hours” post | Early pilot / discount only if you want learning + reference rights |
| 6 | If paid worked: +20% test on same creative+LP. If not: **0 paid**, more organic | Shorter “5 min Loom for product-native buyers” (variant G style) |
| 7 | Ask any happy user for **one** quote; no logo wall required | Onboarding check-in; ask for intro to one peer |
| 8 | **Retro** (see checklist); one decision: “next 60 days = channel X, message Y” | Thank-you to everyone who took a call, even if no deal |

**Calls/week target:** if you can do **0–2** real discovery calls in busy build weeks, that’s a sustainable cadence. **Bursts** (e.g. 4 in one week after a post) are fine.

## Month 1 — foundation + first signals (solo details)

### Marketing (prioritized)

1. **Instrumentation** (your wheelhouse)  
   - UTM on every link you control; one conversion: trial and/or “book 20 min” (or Calendly with **buffer**).  
   - Log variant in a **sheet** if full A/B infra is not ready yet.

2. **Launch surface**  
   - One good LP + pricing path + how to contact. Technical buyers notice **fast pages and clear product screenshots**—use your dev skills.

3. **Warm launch** (free, high impact)  
   - List **20–50** people who might give **honest** feedback (not all buyers). Message: *specific* ask (“15 min, I show invoice→pay flow; you tell me if I’m crazy in PL/EU”).

4. **Organic**  
   - **1–2** LinkedIn posts/week **max** if that’s all you can sustain. One strong post > five meh ones. You can write as **dev founder**: architecture of the *problem*, not startup clichés.

5. **Paid (Tier 1+; Tier 3 = €1–2k/mo see [Money budget](#money-budget-rough-tiers--you-pick-one-lane))**  
   - **Weeks 1–2:** usually **one** platform, **one** geo, daily caps, **2–3 week** review before killing. If CPL is ugly but *two* leads were perfect ICP, note **qualitative** success too.  
   - **Tier 3:** add **retargeting** in week 2–3 once the pixel has data; keep **~75%+** of spend on the main thesis, not 6 micro-tests.

6. **Partners**  
   - **One** real coffee/call with someone who can refer (accountant, ex-coworker in ops). No “partner program” doc needed.

### Sales (you = sales)

- **Form / booking:** 2–3 qualification fields (contractor count, country, B2B invoices y/n). Reject or redirect **politely** the wrong fits.  
- **Call length:** 15–25 minutes default; “send Loom + async email” for tire-kickers.  
- **No enterprise theatre:** no 40-slide deck; **wedge** demo in product ([LANDING-AB](./LANDING-AB-STRATEGIES.md#variant-g--product-led-the-loop-in-one-visual)).  
- **Follow-up:** 2–3 email templates you paste and personalize—enough. No fancy sequence tool required on day 1.

### Month 1 success (solo)

- Funnel not broken.  
- **5+** meaningful interactions (not all “deals”) with notes.  
- You know your **top 3 objections** in your own words.

## Month 2 — double down + one proof (solo details)

- **If** Tier 0 only: all **organic + network**; one **deeper** article or a **public** Loom you can reuse in sales.  
- **If** you had paid winners: +budget **only** on the winning **ad + LP** pair; still cap **2** platforms.  
- **If you’re on ~€1–2k/mo:** second month is **scale or refine**, not a new country—bigger daily caps on the winning set, or **challenger** copy (A vs B) on the **same** channel.  
- **Case / quote:** one paragraph from a user + screenshot you’re allowed to show = enough “social proof v1.”  
- **Webinar / PR / G2:** only if energy is there; **not** default for a solo 60d launch.  
- **Retargeting:** on **Tier 3**, turn on in **weeks 2–4** with simple creative once base traffic exists; on lower tiers, only if the setup is done and you’ll actually look at results weekly.

## Channel tactics — solo-optimized

| Channel | When to use | Time per week (typical) |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------|
| Direct / warm | **Always** first | 1–3 h (messages + calls) |
| LinkedIn organic | Steady, low volume | 1–2 h |
| One niche community | If you’re already there | 0.5–1 h |
| Google / LinkedIn paid | Tier 1+; at **~€1–2k/mo** often **1 primary + retarget** | 1–2 h *including* weekly optimization |
| SEO | 1 good page when you can; no daily blog requirement | 1 h sporadic |
| Email list | 1–2 total broadcasts in 60d if you have a list | 0.5 h each |

**Use your dev advantage:** great **Core Web Vitals**, accessible forms, `mailto`/calendar that works, **no** broken mobile signup—kills trust faster than a weak headline.

## A/B and campaign calendar (simplified for solo)

| When | What | Notes |
|------|------|--------|
| W1–2 | Default LP; sheet log of source | Fix leaks before spending |
| W3–4 | **Two** message tests (A vs B or A vs C on PL) **or** two traffic sources, not 5 tests | |
| W5+ | If paid: more budget **only** on what got **qualified** interest | If no paid: more warm intros + content |
| W7–8 | CTA: trial vs book call on the **winning** page (simple split) | |

When you have more bandwidth, use the **full** A/B calendar (second table, same file below)—treat the **simple table above** as your default.

### A/B and campaign calendar (full — when you have bandwidth)

| When | What to test | Where |
|------|----------------|--------|
| W1–2 | Default LP; fix leaks | All traffic |
| W2–4 | **Hero A vs B** (or **A vs C** on PL only) | Paid + organic links use consistent UTMs |
| W4 | Retargeting *if you run paid+have traffic* | Meta/LinkedIn |
| W5–6 | **Segment LP D** vs generic (agency ad set) | Only if you run paid and have a segment page |
| W7–8 | CTA: trial vs demo on **winner** | 50/50 or time-split |

Log in a **sheet**: date, source, variant, spend (if any), sessions, signups, *qualified* count, notes.

## Sales: sequences (lightweight)

**Trial signup (work email):**  
- Day 0: short personal email or in-app: “3 steps in week one.”  
- Day 3–5: one question, offer **15 min** or **Loom** link you already recorded.

**Demo:**  
- Pre-call: 2 min read their site / LinkedIn.  
- On call: wedge only; [variant E / F / C](./LANDING-AB-STRATEGIES.md) talk if they open with that.

**No time for calls:**  
- **Loom** + “Reply with 2 sentences on your process”—filters serious people.

---

## Metrics (5–15 min / week, spreadsheet)

- Visitors by **source** (if low traffic, **don’t** stare daily—weekly is enough).  
- **Count** of qualified convos and **outcome** (learn / pilot / no / later).  
- If paid: **spend** and **one** efficiency number you care about (e.g. cost per *qualified* lead, not just click).

**North star for solo:** *Are the right people booking or writing back?* not raw traffic.

## Risks (solo-specific)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Time bankruptcy** (GTM + product + support) | Hard **ICP** disqualification; default **async**; **office hours** 1 block/week |
| **Comparing to funded competitors’ marketing** | Your story: **depth and specificity** for one segment |
| **Spending to feel progress** | Cap monthly; if learning is **zero** after 2 weeks, **stop** the ad, keep the product |
| **Loneliness in sales** | Peer founder group or one accountability buddy; **not** required for v1 |
| **You hate LinkedIn** | **Skip**; double down on **communities, email, or search** you’ll actually do |

## End of week 8 — retro checklist

- [ ] **One** message and **one** channel to continue for next 60d (or “product only for 4 weeks” is a valid result).  
- [ ] Top **3** objections in **your** words—update LANDING-AB and site FAQ.

- [ ] If you spent on ads: **decision** to spend again **yes/no** and **why** (data + gut).
