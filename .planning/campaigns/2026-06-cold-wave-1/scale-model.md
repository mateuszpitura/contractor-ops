# 6-month scale model — what "5k per market" really takes

> CMO read on scaling. The instinct — high volume × partners × discounts × content compounding — is directionally right and the destination is real. But "5k per market over 6 months" hits four hard constraints, and the binding one isn't sending. This doc sizes the scale realistically so you build a pipeline you can actually convert, not a firehose that burns the list.

## What's right (affirm it)

- **Multi-channel compounding is the correct model.** Cold + partners + content/SEO + discounts/deadline stacked beats any single channel. Content and SEO especially compound — they don't decay like cold and have no per-send reputation ceiling.
- **The destination is achievable.** A real book of business (dozens of paying customers, €20–60k+ MRR) in 6–12 months across these markets is realistic — IF the mix and the constraints are handled.
- **Partners + discounts + deadline are the accelerants** that pull conversion above cold baseline. Right to layer them.

## The four constraints "5k/market" hits

### 1. Deliverability infra (sending ceiling)
1 warmed inbox safely sends ~30–40 cold/day ≈ 600–800/mo. "5k/market × 6 markets" = 30k over 6 months = ~5k/mo = **needs ~7–8 inboxes minimum, realistically 10–15** with rotation + buffer. That's more domains, more 3-week warmups, more Workspace seats. Doable, but a cost + setup step-change — not free.

### 2. TAM / list quality (the list gets worse as you scale)
You can *find* 5k companies per market; the **ICP-fit + verified-email + trigger-matched** subset is much smaller. PL software-houses ≈ a few thousand total — 5k cold there means scraping the bottom of the barrel. As list quality drops, reply rate collapses toward the low end (0.2%). **5k low-quality often yields the same absolute closes as 1.5k high-quality — while burning the list + reputation.**

### 3. Cost step-change
5k/mo enriched leads blows past Apollo Basic (2,500 credits/mo) → Apollo Org / Clay-at-scale + 10–15 inboxes + verifier volume = **~$500–1,500/mo**, not the lean $124. High cold volume is not a cheap lever.

### 4. THE BINDING ONE — founder follow-up capacity
This is the real ceiling. The math:
- 30k sent × ~2% meeting rate = **~600 meetings over 6 months = ~25/week = ~5/day.**
- A solo founder **cannot** take 5 demos/day AND build product AND run the campaign AND onboard the customers who close.
- Realistic solo capacity: **~10–15 quality meetings/week max** (while also building).

So sending volume that generates more meetings than you can take is **wasted + harmful** — leads sit, go cold, and you've burned reputation + spend to create a backlog you can't work.

## The reframe: size the top of funnel to YOUR conversion capacity

The constraint is not "how many can I send" — it's **"how many replies can I convert."** Work backward from the human bottleneck:

- Founder ceiling ≈ **10–15 quality meetings/week.**
- To fill that, you need ~500–750 *well-targeted* touches/week across ALL channels — not a 5k/market firehose.
- Everything above that ceiling should come from channels that **don't consume founder-per-lead time**: content/SEO (inbound), partner referrals (pre-qualified), and eventually a hire.

## The channel-mix shift over 6 months (the actual plan)

Cold should *decline as a share* while compounding channels grow — because cold is a treadmill (decays, per-send cost, reputation ceiling) and content/partner compound.

| Month | Cold (founder-fed) | Content/SEO | Partners | Notes |
|-------|--------------------|-------------|----------|-------|
| 1–2 | 70% | 10% | 20% | cold proves segments; content seeded; first partners |
| 3–4 | 50% | 25% | 25% | content ranking; partner referrals flowing; cold targets best cells only |
| 5–6 | 35% | 35% | 30% | compounding channels carry the load; cold is precision-only |

By month 6, **the majority of pipeline should NOT be raw cold** — it should be inbound + referral, which convert higher and don't hit the founder-per-lead ceiling.

## What 6 months done right looks like (realistic, not firehose)

- **Cold:** ~1.5–2.5k *high-quality, trigger-matched* per priority market (not 5k spray) = ~8–12k total, ~6–10 inboxes. Targeted at the founder's meeting capacity.
- **Content/SEO:** 12–20 articles compounding → organic pipeline growing month over month (the cheapest per-lead channel).
- **Partners:** 5–10 signed → warm referral flow that converts 2–3× cold.
- **Discounts/deadline:** design-partner pricing + KSeF/1099 deadline as the conversion accelerant (see discount note below).
- **Result:** a pipeline sized to what one founder can convert, fed by the cheapest-converting channels, with cold as ONE input — not the whole engine.

Expected: low tens of paying customers by month 6, climbing as content/partner compound — without burning the addressable list or the sender reputation.

## Discount strategy (use it as an accelerant, carefully)

- **Design-partner / founding-customer pricing** (e.g. 30–50% off year 1, locked) for the first 10–20 — buys logos, references, case studies, product feedback. High ROI early.
- **Deadline-tied urgency** ("lock founding pricing before KSeF") compresses cycles honestly.
- **Guardrail:** discount on e-invoicing/ops (deterministic value), time-box it, and don't anchor your price floor too low — discounts are for *early proof*, not a permanent position. Avoid training the market that the product is cheap.

## The trap

Equating "we can send 5k" with "we'll close proportionally." Sending scales linearly and cheaply; **converting does not** — it's capped by the founder until you hire. Over-sending past that cap burns list + reputation + spend to build a backlog you can't work. Scale the *compounding* channels (content/SEO/partner) past the founder ceiling; keep *cold* sized to what you can personally convert.

## When raw volume actually unlocks

5k/market becomes the right move once you have: (a) a hire / SDR handling replies + meetings, (b) proven copy + offer (post-W6 data), (c) a self-serve onboarding path so closes don't all need a founder demo, (d) the inbox infra + budget. Until then, **precision over volume.**
